Rugby

Description of Rugby

Rugby is a contact team sport played with an oval-shaped ball, where two teams of 15 players each compete to score points by carrying, passing, and kicking the ball over the opposing team's goal line. The game is physically demanding, combining elements of strength, speed, and strategy, with two main variants: Rugby Union and Rugby League. Rugby Union is characterized by scrums, lineouts, and rucks, while Rugby League has a faster pace and emphasizes continuous play with fewer stoppages. Popular in countries like New Zealand, Australia, South Africa, England, and France, rugby has a passionate following and international competitions such as the Rugby World Cup. Known for its camaraderie and sportsmanship, rugby fosters a strong sense of community both on and off the field, contributing to its enduring popularity in various regions.
